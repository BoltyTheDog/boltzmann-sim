Theory

The Lattice Boltzmann Method

The Lattice Boltzmann Method (LBM) is a computational fluid dynamics technique used to simulate fluid flows. Instead of solving the Navier-Stokes equations directly, LBM models fluid as a collection of particles and simulates their collisions and streaming.

This method is particularly well-suited for complex geometries, multiphase flows, and flows in porous media. It's also inherently parallelizable, making it efficient for modern computing architectures.

Key Components of LBM

  • Discretized Velocity Space: The D2Q9 model used here has 9 discrete velocity directions.
  • Collision Step: Particles at each lattice site collide, resulting in redistribution according to the BGK approximation.
  • Streaming Step: After collision, particles move to neighboring lattice sites.
  • Boundary Conditions: Special rules handle obstacles and inlet/outlet conditions.

Reynolds Number

The Reynolds number (Re) characterizes the flow and is given by Re = (characteristic length × velocity) / viscosity. Lower viscosity results in higher Re and potentially turbulent flow patterns.

Formulas

Key Equations

Equilibrium Distribution

The equilibrium distribution function is given by:

f_i^{eq}(\mathbf{x}, t) = w_i \rho \left[ 1 + \frac{\mathbf{e}_i \cdot \mathbf{u}}{c_s^2} + \frac{(\mathbf{e}_i \cdot \mathbf{u})^2}{2c_s^4} - \frac{\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{u}}{2c_s^2} \right]

Collision Operator (BGK Approximation)

The collision step follows:

f_i(\mathbf{x}, t + \Delta t) = f_i(\mathbf{x}, t) - \frac{1}{\tau} [f_i(\mathbf{x}, t) - f_i^{eq}(\mathbf{x}, t)]

Where τ is the relaxation time related to viscosity: τ = 3ν + 0.5

Streaming Step

After collision, distribution functions stream to neighboring nodes:

f_i(\mathbf{x} + \mathbf{e}_i\Delta t, t + \Delta t) = f_i^*(\mathbf{x}, t)

Macroscopic Quantities

Density and velocity are computed from distribution functions:

\rho(\mathbf{x}, t) = \sum_i f_i(\mathbf{x}, t)
\rho(\mathbf{x}, t)\mathbf{u}(\mathbf{x}, t) = \sum_i \mathbf{e}_i f_i(\mathbf{x}, t)

About

This simulator implements the Lattice Boltzmann Method (LBM) for computational fluid dynamics. The backend is powered by Go, and this frontend interface allows for interactive parameter adjustment and visualization.

The simulation creates a circular obstacle in the middle of a flow field and simulates fluid flow around it. The color intensity represents the fluid velocity magnitude.

Implementation Details

  • The D2Q9 model with 9 velocity directions is used
  • BGK collision operator with a single relaxation time
  • Periodic boundary conditions on the top and bottom
  • Inlet velocity condition on the left
  • Outflow condition on the right
  • Bounce-back boundary condition for the obstacle
